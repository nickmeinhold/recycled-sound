Photo capture protocols

Reference shoot guides for the Recycled Sound device register. Pick the section that matches the device shape you're capturing - you only need ONE set per device.

One set per shape class. The two BTE protocols below cover the same 6 anatomical orientations - they're not additive. Default to the hand-held naturals flow. The table rig is legacy: kept for reference and for the rare case where you specifically want a clean scale-card shot for register display, but real-world testing showed hand-shots outperformed calibrated rig shots for training data (22/22 naturals worked; ~3 scaled-protocol attempts failed). Non-BTE shapes and accessories ARE additional - take those when you encounter them.
1. BTE hand-held (default) 2. BTE table rig (legacy) 3. Non-BTE shapes 4. Accessories

1. BTE hand-held naturals

Hand-shot photos of BTE aids held naturally between thumb and index finger. This is the recommended protocol for training data: natural hand-shots match the deployment distribution better than calibrated rig shots.

Why this beats the table rig: in real testing, hand-shot naturals outperformed calibrated table-rig capture for training data. The scanner sees devices in volunteers' hands, not on tables - so train it that way. 22/22 naturals worked; ~3 scaled-protocol attempts failed.
Shoot order: L aid first (all 6 orientations 1-6), then R aid (7-12). 10-12 shots per aid total, with slight angle variation between shots - natural variation helps the model.
Anatomical naming: medial = inner (touches head, label-side); lateral = outer; anterior = front edge; posterior = back edge; superior = top end (hook); inferior = bottom end (shell base).
Lighting: indoor diffuse light. Avoid harsh shadows from overhead bulbs - face a window or use a soft lamp.
Framing: phone camera at arm's length, device fills roughly 60% of frame. Hand is the scale reference - no card needed.

1. Lateral face up

L: shots 1-2.R: shots 7-8
Outer shell faces camera. No label visible.
Hand grip view

2. BTE table rig (legacy - skip unless you need a scale-card reference)

6 orientations x 2 aids (L then R) = 12 shots per pair. Device on the table, credit card in frame as scale reference. Camera always straight down.

Legacy protocol. Use the hand-held flow instead - it covers the same 6 anatomical orientations and beat this rig in real testing. Only fire this protocol when you specifically need a calibrated scale-card shot (e.g. for register display where the audiologist wants a clean overhead photo). Do NOT shoot this AND the hand-held set on the same device - they're redundant.
Shoot order: Always L aid first (all 6 orientations 1-6), then R aid (all 6 orientations 7-12). Same anatomical axis sequence both times.
Anatomical naming: medial = inner (touches head, label-side); lateral = outer; anterior = front edge; posterior = back edge; superior = top end (hook); inferior = bottom end (shell base).

Inner side up. Label visible.

Card visible in every shot. Some orientations are physically awkward - BTEs don't balance well on edges or ends - just hold them with a finger and shoot whatever silhouette you can.

3. Non-BTE shapes - RIC, ITE, CIC, IIC

Smaller in-ear devices. Different anatomy, different shot lists. Hand-held naturals for all; plain background where possible.

General rules: hand-held, indoor diffuse light, fill ~60% of frame. Use macro mode if your phone supports it for CIC/IIC. Coin for scale on IIC/CIC if you want a reference (optional).

RIC (Receiver-in-Canal)

9 shots per aid
Looks like a BTE but with a thin wire ending in a receiver-dome that sits in the ear canal.

Body (BTE-style): all 6 BTE orientations from the hand-held protocol above (lateral, medial, anterior, posterior, superior, inferior).

Receiver bud:

  • Shot 7 - receiver front (looking at the dome end)
  • Shot 8 - receiver side profile

Wire:

  • Shot 9 - full wire path from body to receiver, laid flat
Pinch the body to expose the wire-exit point in shot 9; keep wire from kinking.

ITE (In-the-Ear)

4 shots per aid
Custom-moulded faceplate, fills the concha. No external wire.
  • Shot 1 - faceplate up (controls, vent, mic ports visible)
  • Shot 2 - canal side (the side that goes into the ear, receiver port visible)
  • Shot 3 - lateral profile (side-on, faceplate edge-on left)
  • Shot 4 - posterior profile (rotated 180 degrees from #3)
Hold by the canal end so the faceplate is unobstructed in shot 1. The faceplate is where the brand/serial usually prints.

CIC (Completely-in-Canal)

3 shots per aid
Tiny, sits fully in the canal. Removal handle (clear filament) usually visible.
  • Shot 1 - faceplate end (looking at the outer end, removal handle visible)
  • Shot 2 - side profile (long axis horizontal)
  • Shot 3 - close-up of removal handle / pull-cord
Use macro mode if available. A coin in frame adds scale reference (optional).

IIC (Invisible-in-Canal)

2 shots per aid
Deepest-fitting style. Smaller than CIC. Often no visible controls.
  • Shot 1 - faceplate end (outer end, may show removal filament)
  • Shot 2 - canal end (inner end, receiver port)
Macro mode strongly recommended. Plain matte background (paper, cloth) so the device silhouette is clean.

4. Accessories

Non-aid items in the register: chargers, charging cases, domes, wax filters, tubing, remote controls. Plain background, hand-held or flat.

General rule per accessory: 2 shots - top view + side profile, on a plain background. Special cases below add shots.
End of session: one group shot of any matched set (e.g. charger + aid + charging cable together).

Chargers (standalone)

2 shots
Wall chargers, USB bricks, charging pads.
  • Shot 1 - top view (logos, model markings)
  • Shot 2 - side profile (port, cable exit)

Charging cases

4 shots
Combined charger + storage case for the aids.
  • Shot 1 - closed, top view
  • Shot 2 - closed, side profile
  • Shot 3 - open, empty (charging contacts visible)
  • Shot 4 - open, with aids seated
Keep case on a flat plain surface; camera straight down for shots 1 and 3/4.

Domes

2 shots + scale
Silicone tips for RIC/BTE receivers (open, closed, power, double).
  • Shot 1 - top view (opening visible if vented)
  • Shot 2 - side profile
  • Include a coin in frame (10c or 20c piece) for scale - domes are tiny.

Wax filters

2 shots + scale
Tiny replaceable filters that sit at the receiver outlet.
  • Shot 1 - top view of the carrier/wheel (filters in slots)
  • Shot 2 - single filter close-up
  • Coin in frame for scale.
Macro mode strongly recommended.

Tubing

2 shots
Slim tubes or standard tubes that connect BTE hook to ear mould.
  • Shot 1 - laid flat, full length (note coil if pre-curved)
  • Shot 2 - close-up of connector end

Remote controls

2 shots
Hand-held remotes that pair with aids.
  • Shot 1 - top view (buttons, display, brand)
  • Shot 2 - back / battery compartment

Matched set group shot

1 shot
Final shot of the session for any matched accessory + aid set.
  • Arrange charger, cable, aids, and any included accessories on a plain background.
  • Top-down camera view; everything in one frame.
This shot documents what came as a kit - useful for the donor record and the audiologist's QA check.

Naming and storage: raw photos go to data/photos_nick_aids/<DEVICE_ID>/. Anatomical orientation is encoded in the manifest.json sidecar, not the filename - that way we can re-tag without renaming files.
